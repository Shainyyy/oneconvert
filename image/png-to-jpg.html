When to Convert PNG to JPG?

Convert PNG to JPG when you need smaller file sizes for web publishing, email attachments or social media. JPG is universally supported and produces much smaller files for photographic content. Note that transparent areas will be filled with white, as JPG does not support transparency.

PNG files use lossless compression which keeps every pixel detail — great for logos and screenshots, but can make photos unnecessarily large. JPG uses lossy compression that reduces file size dramatically while maintaining good visual quality for photographs. Note that JPG does not support transparency: transparent areas in the PNG are filled with white in the output.
